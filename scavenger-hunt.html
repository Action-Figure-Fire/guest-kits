🗺️ SCAVENGER HUNT — SAMPLE

Charleston Explorer

Downtown Charleston, SC — Walking Adventure

10 stops. Hidden gems. Local secrets. 2-3 hours on foot. Best played in teams of 2-4.

📋 Rules

How to play: Visit each stop in order. Complete the challenge. Earn points. Take photos as proof. Highest score wins.

Bonus points are available at most stops for going above and beyond. Total possible: 200 points.

Tie-breaker: Fastest completion time wins.

📍 The Hunt

1
The Battery & White Point Garden
2 Murray Blvd — Start here

Where cannons watch the harbor and oak trees have seen centuries. The most photographed spot in Charleston.

📸 Photo Challenge
Find the cannon pointing toward Fort Sumter. Take a group photo pretending to fire it.
10 pts
⭐ Bonus

Count the cannons in the park. Exact number = 10 bonus pts.

2
Rainbow Row
83-107 East Bay St — 5 min walk

13 pastel-colored historic houses. Originally merchant shops in the 1700s, painted to help drunk sailors find their way home. Seriously.

🧩 Riddle
"I'm the color of the sea on a calm day, and I sit between two warm neighbors. What house number am I?"
15 pts
3
The Old Exchange & Provost Dungeon
122 East Bay St — 2 min walk

Built in 1771. George Washington was entertained here. Pirates were imprisoned in the dungeon below. The building has layers — literally.

🔍 Find It
Find the historical plaque on the east wall. What year was the building completed? Text it to the game master.
10 pts
4
Waterfront Park & Pineapple Fountain
1 Vendue Range — 6 min walk

The pineapple is Charleston's symbol of hospitality. This fountain is the most Instagrammed spot in the city.

📸 Photo Challenge
Recreate the "jumping in front of the fountain" photo. Most creative jump wins.
15 pts
⭐ Bonus

Find a local to take your photo AND tell you one fact about the park. +10 pts

5
Church Street — Hidden Alley
Between 89 & 91 Church St

Charleston is full of "single houses" — one room wide, built sideways to catch the breeze. Between two of them is an alley most tourists walk right past.

🔍 Find It
Find the hidden garden gate between 89 and 91 Church Street. What color is it? Photo proof required.
20 pts — This one's tricky
6
City Market
188 Meeting St — 5 min walk

Running for over 200 years. The sweetgrass basket weavers here practice an art form passed down from West African ancestors through the Gullah Geechee culture.

🗣️ Talk to a Local
Find a sweetgrass basket weaver. Ask how long it takes to make one basket. Report back with the answer.
15 pts
7
Blind Tiger Pub
36 Broad St — 7 min walk

One of the oldest bars in Charleston. During Prohibition, bars advertised "come see our blind tiger" — code for "we serve booze." The name stuck.

🍺 Refreshment Stop
Order something. Take a photo of your drink with the bar sign visible. Ask the bartender for their favorite Charleston secret.
10 pts + hydration
8
St. Michael's Church
71 Broad St — 1 min walk

Built in 1761. The steeple has been painted black (to hide it from enemy ships), white, and once — accidentally — the wrong shade of cream, which caused a city-wide scandal.

🧩 Riddle
"I ring every quarter hour and have survived wars, earthquakes, and hurricanes. I was cast in London. How many bells do I have in my tower?"
15 pts
9
King Street Shopping District
King St between Broad & Market

The most walkable shopping street in the South. Upper King = restaurants, Middle King = fashion, Lower King = antiques.

📸 Photo Challenge
Find the most unusual storefront on King Street. Photo it. The group votes on whose find is best.
15 pts
10
Finish: Callie's Hot Little Biscuit
476 King St

You've earned it. The best biscuits in the South. This is not up for debate.

🏆 Final Challenge
Order the signature biscuit. Rate it 1-10. Take a group victory photo. You're done!
15 pts
